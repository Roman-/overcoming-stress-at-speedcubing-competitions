# Overcoming Nerves in Speedcubing Competitions

🎥 Video: https://youtu.be/98y88lXtafA  
🧠 Mind Map (XMind): https://app.xmind.com/Ngc9KfTN  

---

## The Problem

You practice a lot at home.  
Your average is good.  

Then you go to a **cubing competition**.

Your hands shake.  
Your heart rate goes up.  
Your solve is worse than usual.

Why?

Because competition solves are **not your average situation**.

They are:
- Noisy
- Unfamiliar
- Public
- Limited (only 5 solves)
- High pressure

Your brain switches from **normal mode** to **panic mode**.

---

## The Core Idea

You cannot change the competition environment.

But you can change your **home practice**.

The goal is simple:

> Make your home solves psychologically and physically similar to competition solves.

Remove novelty.  
If nothing feels new, your brain stays calm.

---

## Two Buckets of the Framework

### 1️⃣ Objective (Environment)

Things that physically change at competition:

- People watching you
- Background noise
- Random waiting times
- Different lighting
- Different table height
- Different chair
- WCA routine (scorecard signing, judge, etc.)
- Limited number of solves

### 2️⃣ Subjective (Psychology)

Things happening in your head:

- Spotlight feeling
- Expectations
- Fear of failure
- Overthinking
- Treating the solve as “special”

---

## High Impact Techniques

### ✅ Stream Your Solves

Turn on a camera before practice.

Even if nobody watches:
- There is always a chance someone joins.
- You get used to being observed.
- Being watched becomes normal.

---

### ✅ Add Competition Noise

- Play a recording of a big competition in the background.
- Let people walk on screen in your peripheral vision.
- Get used to movement and noise.

---

### ✅ Randomize Variables

Before practice, change something:

- Table height
- Chair position
- Light source position
- Distance from table
- Temperature
- Random delay before starting

Make every session slightly different.

Your brain learns:
> “Different is normal.”

---

### ✅ Simulate Official Rounds

Create 5 solves per day that fully simulate competition:

- Random wait time
- Scorecard
- No extra attempts
- Strict routine

These are your “competition mode” solves.

---

### ✅ Mental Trick

At competition:
- Don’t think about the result.
- Don’t think about records.
- Don’t think about expectations.

Think about something unrelated:
- After party
- Friends
- Dinner
- Anything positive and external

The goal:
> Treat the solve as just another repetition.

Not special.  
Not important.  
Just one of thousands.

---

## Summary

Nerves and stress in speedcubing competitions come from novelty.

Your brain thinks:
“This is important. This is different. This is dangerous.”

Your job is to prove it wrong.

When competition feels identical to home practice,
your hands stop shaking.

Build your own version of this framework using the mind map.

Pick one variable.
Change it today.
Repeat.

Make competition your norm.
